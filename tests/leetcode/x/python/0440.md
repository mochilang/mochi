View the numbers from 1 to n as a preorder traversal of a decimal trie. For a prefix p, count how many valid numbers are in its subtree by widening the interval [p, p + 1) one decimal level at a time. If that subtree count is not larger than the remaining k offset, skip to the next sibling; otherwise descend to p * 10. This finds the kth lexicographic number without enumerating the range.

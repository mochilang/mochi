Use two pointers from both ends. Keep `left`, `right`, `leftMax`, and `rightMax`. At each step, whichever side has the smaller current maximum is the side we can safely settle, because the opposite side already has a wall at least that tall. If `height[left]` is below `leftMax`, it traps `leftMax - height[left]`; otherwise update `leftMax`. Do the symmetric step on the right side when `rightMax` is smaller. This runs in `O(n)` time and uses `O(1)` extra space.

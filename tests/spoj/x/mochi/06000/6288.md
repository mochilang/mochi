# SPOJ PYRA - Pyramids

https://www.spoj.com/problems/PYRA/

Given base side a, find the volume of a square pyramid with equilateral triangle faces.
The lateral faces are equilateral triangles with side a.

## Approach

The height of an equilateral triangle with side a is sqrt(3)/2 * a.
Place the pyramid with base center at origin. Base vertices are at (+-a/2, +-a/2, 0).
The apex is directly above. Height h satisfies: sqrt((a/2)^2 + (a/2)^2) = apothem of base = a/sqrt(2).
The slant height of a lateral face = sqrt(3)/2 * a (height of equilateral triangle).
h = sqrt(slant^2 - (a/sqrt(2))^2) = sqrt(3a^2/4 - a^2/2) = sqrt(a^2/4) = a/2.
Volume = (1/3) * a^2 * h = a^3/6.
